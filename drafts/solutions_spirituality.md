We don't use the word "Spirituality" lightly. It often means "soft."
For the Integrated Man, Focus is about **Control**.
Control over your attention. Control over your reaction. Control over your mind.

## The Morning Protocol (Prime the System)

Don't wake up and scroll the news. That is letting the world dictate your mood.
**Own the first 30 minutes.**

1.  **Hydrate:** (See Toolkit 5).
2.  **Move:** (See Toolkit 2).
3.  **Silence:** 10 minutes of nothing. No phone. No TV. Just coffee and the window.
    *   This builds the muscle of "sitting with yourself."
    *   If you can sit in silence, you don't need to numb the noise.

## The Stoic Pause

Between the **Stimulus** (traffic, rude comment, bad news) and the **Response** (anger, drinking, yelling), there is a space.
Alcohol erases that space. Sobriety widens it.

**The Tactic:** When triggered, count to 5.
*   Breathe in.
*   Breathe out.
*   *Then* respond.

This is the difference between a reaction (child) and a response (man).

## Digital Hygiene

Your phone is a dopamine dispenser. It competes with your peace.
*   **No News Apps:** If it's important, you'll hear about it.
*   **No Phone in the Bedroom:** Buy an alarm clock.
*   **Gray Scale:** Turn your phone screen to black and white. It makes it boring.

## Gratitude as Data

Gratitude isn't woo-woo. It's **Pattern Recognition**.
Your brain is wired to see threats (survival). You have to train it to see wins.

**The Nightly Audit:**
Before you sleep, name three things that went well.
*   "The coffee was good."
*   "The workout was hard."
*   "My wife laughed."

This rewires your bias from negative to positive.

## Summary
*   Own the morning.
*   Practice the Stoic Pause.
*   Get the phone out of the bedroom.
*   Audit the wins.
